carrots

Carrots are herbaceous, generally biennial plants that produce an edible taproot. They are commonly grown as annual plants and can be eaten fresh or cooked. The roots are a good source of the nutrient carotene.

cauliflower

Cauliflower is a cruciferous vegetable1that belongs to the Brassicaceae family. It is a highly modified form of cabbage, grown for its edible masses of partially developed flower structures and fleshy stalks. The white head is the only edible part, and the leaves are there to protect it from the dirt3. The green stems may also be consumed if they're tender. Cauliflower is high in vitamins C and K and is frequently served as a cooked vegetable.

broccoli

Broccoli is a green vegetable with a large flowering head, stalk and small leaves. It belongs to the cabbage family (Brassicaceae) and the species Brassica oleracea. It is an annual or biennial plant that grows upright and branching with leathery leaves.
